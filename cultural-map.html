Tower of Pisa

Built as the bell tower of the cathedral in the Campo dei Miracoli, it began to tilt as early as the third floor due to unstable ground. Standing nearly 56 meters tall and clad in white marble, it is a symbol of Romanesque architecture and Italian resilience, having survived centuries of restoration attempts. Today, recognized as a UNESCO World heritage Site, it continues to fascinate millions of visitors each year.

Fun Facts

  • Pisa is not just about the tower: the Campo dei Miracoli also included the Cathedral, The Baptistery, and the monumental Cemetery, forming a unique architectural ensemble.
  • In the Middel Ages, Pisa was a powerful martime republic, rivaling Venice and Genoa; its wealth financed the grear works that still amaze visitors today.
